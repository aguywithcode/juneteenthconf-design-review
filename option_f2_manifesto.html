1 6 1 9
Stolen.

Twenty and odd arrived at Point Comfort.

Names erased. Languages forbidden. Families broken.

They built everything. Owned nothing.

Two hundred and forty-six years of this.

1 8 6 5
Free on paper.

June nineteenth. Galveston, Texas.

The last to know they were no longer property.

Freedom announced two and a half years late.

Free. But not equal.

1 9 6 4
Demanding.

Marching. Sitting in. Standing up.

Rights written into law that should have been birthright.

Still fighting for a seat at a table we didn't design.
