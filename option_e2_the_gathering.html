The Journey to This Table
1619
The first enslaved Africans arrive at Point Comfort, Virginia

20 and odd Negroes. Torn from home, stripped of name and nation. Four hundred years ago, a story of resilience began.

Middle Passage
Image Placeholder
1865
"All enslaved people are… absolutely equal"

June 19th, Galveston, Texas. General Order No. 3. Two and a half years after the Emancipation Proclamation, the last enslaved people in Texas finally learn they are free. The chains break. Juneteenth is born.

General Order No. 3

1964
We shall not be moved

From sit-ins to the March on Washington, from Selma to the Civil Rights Act. A generation demanded a seat at the table — and was told to wait. They didn't wait.

2000s
The digital frontier

Black engineers, founders, and visionaries build the technologies that power the world — while fighting for representation in the rooms where decisions are made. The seat at the table was never enough.

2020
We built our own table.

A pandemic. A reckoning. The world proclaimed Black Lives Matter while conferences still claimed "Black talent is hard to find." We filled a roster of brilliant Black speakers in 16 days. What they said couldn't be done in a year, we did in two weeks. We didn't wait for permission. We built it ourselves.
