Programs

Three programs, one mission — creating space for Black technologists to connect, learn, and grow.

Conference

Two days. 20 sessions. One community.

The Juneteenth Conference is an annual two-day gathering of Black technologists across engineering, data science, security, entrepreneurship, and tech leadership. Curated sessions feature industry leaders sharing hard-won knowledge, with networking and community-building woven throughout.

  • 20 curated sessions across multiple tracks
  • Keynote and fireside chats with industry leaders
  • Attendee dinner cruise and networking events
  • Past speakers include Angie Jones, Jerome Hardaway, Tim Banks, Minda Harts, and Wesley Faulkner

Accelerator

From potential to pipeline.

The Tech Workforce Accelerator provides hands-on project experience, mentorship, and career pipeline support for Black professionals entering or advancing in tech. Participants work on real-world projects with experienced mentors, building portfolios and professional networks.

  • Hands-on project experience with real deliverables
  • Mentorship from senior technologists
  • Career pipeline support and job placement assistance
  • Portfolio building and interview preparation

Mentorship

Guidance that compounds.

The Mentorship Program pairs aspiring technologists with experienced professionals for structured upskilling, portfolio building, and career guidance. Relationships are built to last beyond the program, creating a network of support that grows with each cohort.

  • Structured pairing with experienced professionals
  • Upskilling and career development focus
  • Portfolio building and professional branding
  • Long-term relationship building and alumni network
